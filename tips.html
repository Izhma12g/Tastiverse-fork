1. Keep Your Knives Sharp

A sharp knife is not only more efficient but also safer than a dull one. A dull knife requires more force, increasing the risk of slipping and injuring yourself. Regularly honing your knife with a steel sharpener or getting it professionally sharpened helps ensure precise cuts, making your food prep faster and safer. Remember, a sharp knife also helps maintain the quality of ingredients, especially when slicing delicate items like herbs or tomatoes.

2. Organise Your Workspace

Before diving into any recipe, take a few moments to organise your workspace. This technique, known as “mise en place” (everything in its place), involves laying out all your ingredients, tools, and utensils before starting. Not only does it streamline the cooking process, but it also reduces stress and minimises mistakes, such as forgetting an ingredient or burning something while searching for the right tool.

3. Use Room Temperature Ingredients

Whenever possible, use room temperature ingredients, especially for baking. Cold butter, eggs, or milk can hinder the blending process, leading to uneven texture or flat baked goods. Allow your ingredients to rest at room temperature for about 30 minutes before use. This helps create smoother batters and doughs, leading to better results, whether you're making a cake, cookies, or bread.

4. Taste As You Cook

Always taste your dish at various stages of cooking. This will allow you to adjust the seasoning or add ingredients to balance the flavours. It’s easier to fix a mildly under-seasoned dish early than trying to salvage an overly salty one later.

5. Use a Damp Towel to Secure Your Cutting Board

A cutting board that slips can be dangerous. To avoid this, place a damp towel underneath it to keep it in place while you chop. This simple trick ensures better control over your cutting surface, reducing the risk of accidents.
